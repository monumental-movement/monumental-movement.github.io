    The album includes songs such as the singles “Gloria” and “Fire.” These songs are a testament to U2’s early sound and their growth, with a vibrant energy and passion.

    “October” was not a commercial success upon its release, but was later reappraised by U2 fans and critics. This album was an important step in U2’s exploration of their musical direction and the development of their own unique style.

    “October” reflects U2’s early creative output and occupies an important place in their career. The album was supported by many fans for its unique sound and deep lyrics, and became the foundation for U2 to establish itself as a global rock band.
